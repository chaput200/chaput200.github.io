This quiet town still searches for answers

May 2, 2025 Gloria Finch

It’s been a quarter-century since a seemingly innocent bake sale at Fairgrove Elementary turned into a medical mystery that stunned the nation. 43 attendees were hospitalized after consuming brownies tainted with an unknown toxin. No one has ever been charged.

“It felt like a normal day,” recalls Leslie Chang, then a third grader. “I bought a cookie, then the room just started spinning.”

Despite dozens of interviews, a forensic investigation, and national media attention at the time, the case remains officially open. The FBI concluded the contamination was deliberate, yet no conclusive suspect has been identified.

Local folklore now surrounds the incident. Some blame a disgruntled lunch staffer. Others claim it was part of a secret government experiment. Most chilling is the anonymous note found at the scene, which simply read: “Sweet revenge is best served baked.”

New efforts by independent podcasters and amateur sleuths have reignited interest. The Fairgrove Historical Society recently digitized old police files, hoping modern tech might uncover a long-buried clue.
