E101 Final Project

Have you ever been stranded on the side of the road and need to change your tire, but you encounter the warning traffic triangle not being noticeable to warn other drivers on the road to slow down? We were presented with the idea that we needed to create a useful item for someone else. After expanding upon that, we decided to create a more improved traffic warning triangle to try and avoid the situation above. This would redefine our problem statement from ‘create a useful device for someone else’ into ‘create a light-up, useful, warning triangle’.

When trucks or cars break down on the road, there is always a traffic jam that follows. We have to ask ourselves why does this always occur? The most common reason is because drivers are unaware of the stuck vehicle and merge late. These late merges can cause aggressive drivers to swerve around and cause other potential accidents. What is a driver supposed to do when they break down anyway? No one carries flares any more because they are too dangerous, and a car’s hazard lights aren't enough warning. Most young people don’t even have an idea of what a warning triangle is because they do not work effectively in getting a driver’s attention. This is a problem that needs to be fixed. That is why our project is so useful! Every driver can pull out a lighted warning triangle that actually gets the attention of other drivers.

To create our product we need to laser cut a hollow triangle with a slot to fit LED lights must be made. The micro bit will be the backbone to our product. The bit creates a function that will run three options for the triangle’s lights (solid red, red and orange flashing, solid orange) and will have a function that allows the tool to be turned on and off to save battery. Finally there needs to have a cover to keep the lights dry, a transparent plastic must be laser cut to fit over the front of our triangle. Materials that we will need (that we do not already have) will be plastic sheet to laser cut, and lights (potentially)

Materials:

  • Leds 
  • Plastic (to laser cut to form the triangle)
  • 3D print box (to hold microbit)
  • Clear plastic (Cover to make it waterproof for lights and microbit)
  • Screws etc.

How to put it together

  • Battery is plugged into breadboard
  • Micro bit with breakout solder then plug into breadboard
  • Solder wire to lights then plug into breadboard
  • Put solder on lights then on each wire then touch wire to lights to connect easy
  • Connect a few pixels first to check with how power works and mess with code
  • Ignore clock to wire and test
  • Cannot use b/c output is double the amount due to the clock and data. Three wire is easier b/c already code -- potentially

3 wire lights

  • Red = +volt
  • Green = data
  • Blue = ground
  • 3 wires into breadboard = simple

Power source

  • One AA battery = 3.9 watts, 2.7 amp/hour, 1.5 volt
  • .3 max watts, 5 volts needed per pixel
  • 3.9 / .3 watts per pixel = max of 13 pixels per AA
  • 4 AA batteries = 10.8 amp/hours
  • Power = volt * current is battery

NOTE:

Laser cut circles or drill circles avery few inches to insert 3 wire pixels and use a 3d printed box to hold the sheet with lights upward. Inside the box put a micro bit.
